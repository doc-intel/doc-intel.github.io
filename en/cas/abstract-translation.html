Translating a document, term for term

Translating a technical text is not paraphrasing it: the terms, numbers and proper nouns have to survive. The translation runs segment by segment, with the source kept alongside to check.

The problem

A document to read in another language. A general translator sometimes shifts a technical term, rounds a number, or rephrases a sentence enough to move its meaning. On a document that will be relied on, that is not acceptable.

The approach

The text is segmented, then translated with a strict instruction: keep technical terms, numbers and proper nouns verbatim, keep paragraph breaks, add nothing. The source stays displayed next to the translation so a reviewer can check each passage; a domain glossary can lock terminology.

The thread: a useful translation is verifiable, source alongside. The terms that carry the meaning do not move.

What it shows

Document translation is a segment-by-segment alignment you can check, not a reworded block. The source alongside and the glossary are what make the result usable where wording matters.

Deliverables

A source-and-translation view of a technical abstract, aligned segment by segment. The document is public.
