The 360 context: gathering all of a policyholder's information

A policyholder is dozens of scattered documents. The 360 context gathers, parses and types them into one up-to-date whole. It is not a chatbot: it is what a chatbot, and many other uses, consume.

The first insurance brick is the 360 view. It comes in four cases: the client 360 chatbot, the general-agent 360 chatbot, the 360 context that gathers everything known about a policyholder, and the 360 contracts view. The two chatbots consume the context; they are not its only output.

The problem

A policyholder is dozens of scattered documents: policies, endorsements, letters, receipts, statements, claim files. They live in several systems (a DMS, mailboxes, scans). Nobody has a complete, up-to-date picture of the policyholder. Yet that picture, that context, is exactly what everything downstream is missing.

The approach

Gather all of a policyholder's information into one clean, typed context. You collect all their documents, parse them whatever the format, type them (policy, endorsement, claim, letter), extract the key entities, and assemble a consolidated, up-to-date context. An AI-enriched DMS. It is not a chatbot: it is the material a chatbot, and many other uses, will consume.

The key point: the 360 context is not just a chatbot brick. It is a foundation. The chatbot uses it, but a dashboard, an audit, a regulatory extraction or a risk score use it just as much.

What the context feeds

The 360 chatbotClient as well as general agent: the context gives it enough to understand and answer well.
The dashboardThe consolidated view of the policyholder for the case handler: policies, claims, due dates.
Audit and complianceThe 360 contracts view and regulatory checks build on the same context.
Extraction and scoringAny downstream processing starts from a clean context rather than scattered raw documents.

Technical vision

The bricks already exist in the series. Multi-format parsing (Volume 2) reads each document. Classification types it. Structured extraction files the entities into a "policyholder" model. Long-format storage keeps every fact tied to its source. Aggregated views rebuild the file. The context is not frozen: a new document comes in, it updates.

Internal dataGoverned access This case works on policyholders' documents: no public demo, the pieces of a file are confidential.

Implementation roadmap

  1. Collect and parsePull a policyholder's existing documents from the source systems and parse them in batch, format by format.
  2. Type and extractClassify each document (policy, endorsement, claim, letter) and extract the key entities.
  3. Policyholder modelFile the extracted facts into a single data model: policies, claims, due dates, contacts.
  4. Expose the contextMake it consumable by the chatbot, the dashboard, the audit, the extraction.
  5. Incremental updateOn each new document, refresh the context without recomputing everything.

What it shows

Before you build a 360 assistant, you need the 360 context. Gathering and typing the information is the real foundation; without it, a chatbot only guesses. And because it is a foundation, it serves well beyond the chatbot.
