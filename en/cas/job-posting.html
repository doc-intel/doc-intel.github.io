Drafting a job posting from a short brief

A one-line brief becomes a structured, ready-to-review job posting, in the house tone, that a recruiter validates before it goes out.

The problem

Writing a good posting from scratch is slow, and postings drift in tone and structure across a team. A short brief holds all the intent; the draft should follow from it, consistently.

The approach

The brief drives a structured draft: title, role, requirements, nice-to-haves, in a consistent house tone. Nothing is published automatically; the recruiter reviews and edits, and the assistant keeps the structure stable across postings.

The thread: the brief drives a structured draft, not a blank page. The recruiter stays in control of what actually goes out.

What it shows

Generation for real use is structured and reviewable: a short intent becomes a consistent draft a human signs off on, rather than an unpredictable block of text.

Deliverables

A structured job-posting draft generated from a one-line brief, ready for a recruiter to review. The example is synthetic.
