Summarizing a full paper into anchored claims

A long paper reduced to a set of claims, section by section, where each claim points back to the lines it came from. A summary you can check, not just read.

The problem

A model summary of a long document reads well but is hard to trust: you cannot tell which sentence came from which part, or whether the model invented a detail. For anything you will rely on, a fluent paragraph is not enough.

The approach

The document is parsed line by line and summarized by section, each claim carrying the lines that support it. The section summaries are aggregated into an overall one without losing the links. A claim that rests on no line is counted as unsupported and flagged.

The thread: a faithful summary keeps every claim tied to the source. Faithfulness becomes a number, not a promise.

What it shows

Summarization for real use is traceable: the reader can walk from any claim back to the text, and the count of unsupported claims measures how faithful the summary is.

Deliverables

A structured summary of a full paper, with claims anchored to their source lines and a count of unsupported claims. The paper is public.
