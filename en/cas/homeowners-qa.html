Answering questions on a US homeowners policy

A homeowner asks what their policy covers. The four RAG bricks turn a long US home-insurance policy into a sourced answer, each one pinned to the exact page.

The problem

A homeowners policy runs dozens of pages. The answer to a precise coverage question is buried in it, and an answer without the page is worthless to the insured or the agent.

The approach

The four bricks run on a public policy: the document is extracted line by line with page numbers, the question is parsed into keywords, retrieval filters the relevant lines, and generation returns a structured answer with the exact citation and page.

The thread: the page is the proof. A useful RAG answer points to the line of the policy that backs it.

What it shows

Enterprise RAG is a chain of four inspectable bricks, not a black box. On a public policy, the same chain answers any coverage question with a citation.
