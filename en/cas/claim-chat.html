Filing an insurance claim by chatbot, field by field

A claim form makes people quit. A conversation that asks one question at a time, keeps track, and only hands back once the required fields are filled, gets finished.

The problem

A claim declaration is twenty fields. Shown at once, the insured abandons it. Asked at random, one always ends up missing. It has to be guided, one question at a time.

The approach

The chatbot targets a typed output schema: at each turn it asks the next missing field, reads the free-text answer into a typed value, and updates the form. When the required fields are all there, it recaps and proposes an estimate.

The thread: the output is a typed object, not a wall of text. The dialogue exists only to fill that data contract, field by field.

What it shows

A good task chatbot is a conversation constrained by a schema. The structure guarantees no required field slips through, and the output feeds the claims system directly.

Deliverables

A guided claim-declaration chatbot, with the typed form filling up as the dialogue goes. The data is fictional.
