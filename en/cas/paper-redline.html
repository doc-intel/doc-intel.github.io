Redlining two versions of a document

Two versions of the same document. A raw diff drowns you in noise. What you want is the substance: what was actually added, removed, or reworded.

The problem

Comparing two revisions by eye is slow; a character diff is noise. The question is which changes matter: a reworded clause, an added paragraph, a removed condition, not a moved comma.

The approach

The two versions are aligned paragraph by paragraph, then compared. Cosmetic differences are set aside; the output is a redline of the substantive changes, each traceable back to where it sits in each version.

The thread: a redline separates substance from cosmetics. You read what changed in meaning, not what moved on the page.

What it shows

Version comparison is an alignment problem, not a text-diff problem. Aligning first turns a noisy diff into a readable list of real changes.

Deliverables

A redline between two versions of a document, showing the substantive additions, deletions and edits.
