The document workstation

A desktop application that indexes a folder of documents and answers your questions, on your machine. The engine runs locally through a Python sidecar.

Every answer shows its process

The "how this answer was produced" panel unfolds the four bricks: document parsing, question parsing, retrieval, generation. Nothing is hidden, everything is replayable.

The document stays at the center

The PDF is shown as-is, with parsed text, annotations and chat alongside. You read the source, you never lose sight of it.

In action

A few scenarios, from corpus-wide chat to typed intents.

How it works

Pick a folder The app scans the folder and finds the documents to process.
Prepare the corpus Each document is parsed into relational tables, classified and indexed.
Ask questions The chat parses the question, filters the corpus, and composes a sourced answer.
Check the process Every answer points back to the exact passages that produced it.

Everything stays local: the index lives in a folder next to the scanned files, nothing leaves for a server.
