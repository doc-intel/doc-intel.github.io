A repair invoice, its total inflated — and the original still inside

Fictional example, synthetic documents. Detected from the file itself, no external lookup.

The scenario. A claims handler receives a repair invoice as a PDF. The total reads $1,800 and clears in ten seconds. But the number was bumped up after the invoice was finished. On screen it looks clean. The file, though, kept the earlier version.

What the file was hiding

The invoice was re-saved after the total was changed. That kind of save leaves the earlier version buried inside the file. We reconstruct it, and the honest total comes back. Here they are, side by side:

How we catch it

1
An issued invoice is not reopened. Produced once by a billing system, it has no reason to be re-saved. This one was.
2
We recover the earlier state. The re-save left the original inside the file. We reconstruct it and make it readable.
3
Two totals, one invoice. Not a guess: the document supplied both numbers. It goes to the handler with the evidence attached.
