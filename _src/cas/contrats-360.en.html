360 view · Contracts · Insurance

Every contract of a policyholder, in one view

One policyholder often holds several contracts, managed in silos. The 360 contracts view brings them together technically, under the regulatory constraints on access and sharing.

The 360 view comes in four cases. The two 360 chatbots (client and general agents) answer and route. The 360 context gathers everything known about a policyholder. The 360 contracts view (this page) is its compliance facet: bringing their contracts together under regulatory constraints.

The problem

One policyholder often holds several contracts: motor, home, health, protection. They are managed in separate systems, sometimes by different entities. Nobody sees the whole relationship at once. And you cannot simply open everything up: each product, each entity, has its own access rules.

The approach

Bring the contract data together into a unified view, while applying the regulatory restrictions. You extract each contract into a common schema, reconcile the contracts of a single policyholder, and place an authorisation layer on top: who is allowed to see what. Centralising the data is not centralising the rights.

Technical vision

A common, multi-product contract schema, fed by extraction from each source system. An identity resolution reconciles the contracts of a single policyholder. An access layer filters every read by role and entity. Every access is traced. The consolidated view only exposes what the user is allowed to see.

Internal dataAccess control The data is centralised technically; the rights, though, stay compartmentalised.

Regulatory constraints

  • Access control: every read filtered by role and entity; no global view by default.
  • Data protection: minimisation, encryption, bounded retention.
  • Sharing rules: sharing between entities follows the regulation, not technical convenience.

Implementation roadmap

  1. Unified contract schemaDefine a common model able to represent the different products.
  2. Per-system extractionFeed the schema from each source system, contract by contract.
  3. Identity resolutionReliably reconcile the contracts of a single policyholder.
  4. Access layer and auditFilter every read by role and entity, trace the accesses.
  5. Consolidated viewExpose the full relationship, within the limits allowed to each user.

What it shows

The 360 contracts view is as much a data-architecture matter as a compliance one. The challenge is not bringing things together, it is bringing them together while respecting the regulatory walls. Technical centralisation and the compartmentalisation of rights are not at odds: they are designed together.
